# CA6.1 — Group Conversation  
## Dinner with fictional characters

**Participants:** Héctor and Joel  
**Platform:** Google Meet  
**Recording:** Joel  
**Topic:** Organising a dinner with fictional characters  
**Duration:** 15–30 minutes approximately  

> **Important:** this script is only for practising and organising ideas.  
> During the recording, it should not be read word by word. It should sound like a natural conversation.

---

# 1. Opening / Introduction

## Héctor

> Hi Joel. I’m Héctor.  
> Today we are going to talk about a dinner party with fictional characters.  
> We have to decide three things: the guests, the food, and an activity after the meal.

## Héctor

> Is that okay for you, Joel?

## Joel

> Yes, that’s fine.

---

# 2. Choosing the fictional guests

## 2.1 Joel presents his character: Harry Potter

## Héctor

> So, Joel, which fictional character would you like to invite?

## Joel

> I would like to invite Harry Potter.

## Héctor

> That sounds interesting.  
> Why did you choose Harry Potter?

## Joel

> I chose Harry Potter because he is brave, kind and he has lived many adventures.  
> I think he would be a very interesting guest.

## Héctor

> Yes, I think Harry Potter is a good choice.  
> He could tell us many interesting stories about magic and his adventures.

---

## 2.2 Héctor presents his character: Spiderman

## Joel

> And you, Héctor? Which fictional character would you like to invite?

## Héctor

> I would like to invite Spiderman.

## Joel

> Why did you choose Spiderman?

## Héctor

> I chose Spiderman because I think he would be a very interesting guest.  
> He is brave, funny and friendly.  
> Also, he has many stories to tell, so the dinner would not be boring.

---

# 3. Comparing Harry Potter and Spiderman

## Héctor

> I think Harry Potter and Spiderman are both good character choices for the dinner.

## Joel

> Why do you think so?

## Héctor

> Because both of them are brave and both try to help other people.  
> They are different, of course, because Harry Potter comes from a magical world and Spiderman is more like a superhero.  
> But they both fight against evil and try to protect other people.

## Héctor

> Do you think they would have things in common?

## Joel

> Yes, I think so. Both of them are young, brave and they have difficult lives.

## Héctor

> Yes, I agree with you.  
> I think they would make a good combination for the dinner.

---

# 4. Choosing a third character

## Héctor

> Maybe we should choose one more character in common.  
> What do you think about Hermione Granger, Joel?  
> Do you know who she is?

---

## Option A — If Joel knows Hermione

## Joel

> Yes, she is Harry Potter’s friend.

## Héctor

> Exactly.  
> I think Hermione could be a good guest because she is clever, curious and very practical.  
> She usually thinks before acting, so she could bring balance to the group.  
> She is Harry Potter’s friend, so she would get along well with him.  
> But I also think she could have an interesting conversation with Spiderman.

---

## Option B — If Joel does not know Hermione

## Joel

> I’m not sure. Who is she?

## Héctor

> She is one of Harry Potter’s best friends.  
> She is clever, curious and very practical.  
> She usually thinks before acting, so she could bring balance to the group.  
> I think she could be a good guest because she would make the conversation more interesting.

---

## Héctor

> Maybe Spiderman would bring the funny and adventurous part, Harry Potter would bring the magical part, and Hermione would bring the intelligent and practical part.

## Héctor

> Do you think Hermione would be a good third guest?

## Joel

> Yes, I think she would be a good choice.

## Héctor

> Great. So we could invite Harry Potter, Spiderman and Hermione Granger.

---

# 5. Deciding the food

## 5.1 Héctor’s proposal: Chinese restaurant

## Héctor

> Now we have to decide the food.  
> For the food, I would suggest going to a Chinese restaurant.  
> I like Chinese food because there are many different dishes, and everyone can choose something different.

## Héctor

> Also, I’m vegan, so I would choose dishes without meat or fish.

---

## 5.2 Héctor’s dishes

## Dish 1 — Tofu with vegetables

## Héctor

> First, I would choose tofu with vegetables.  
> I like it because it is healthy, light and tasty.  
> It is also a good vegan option, and I think it can work well with different sauces.

## Dish 2 — Fried rice

## Héctor

> I would also choose fried rice because it is simple, tasty and it goes well with many different dishes.  
> Most people usually like rice, so I think it would be a safe option for the dinner.

---

# 6. Joel’s food proposal

## Héctor

> What about you, Joel?  
> What type of food would you choose?

## Joel

> I would choose Mexican food.

## Héctor

> Why would you choose Mexican food?

## Joel

> I would choose Mexican food because I like it. It is tasty and full of flavour.

## Héctor

> That sounds good.  
> Which Mexican dishes would you include in the menu?

## Joel

> Maybe fish and chips.

---

# 7. If Joel says fish and chips

## Héctor

> Fish and chips sounds good, but I think it is more British than Mexican.  
> Maybe if we choose Mexican food, we could have tacos, nachos or quesadillas.  
> What do you think?

## Joel

> Yes, maybe tacos or nachos would be better for Mexican food.

## Héctor

> Yes, I think tacos or nachos could work well.  
> They are easy to eat and they are quite informal, so the dinner would feel more relaxed.

## Héctor

> Do you think our characters would enjoy spicy food?

## Joel

> Maybe Harry Potter would like it, but I’m not sure.

## Héctor

> Yes, maybe.  
> But if the food is too spicy, some guests might not enjoy it.  
> Maybe Chinese food could be easier because we can choose different dishes.

---

# 8. Possible agreement about food

## Héctor

> So, maybe we can choose the Chinese restaurant because it has vegan options and different dishes for everyone.

## Joel

> Yes, I think Chinese food is okay.

## Héctor

> Great. Then we can choose Chinese food and include tofu with vegetables and fried rice.

---

# 9. Activity after the meal

## 9.1 Héctor’s proposal: theatre

## Héctor

> Now we have to decide what to do after the meal.  
> After dinner, I would suggest going to the theatre to see *Les Misérables*.  
> I think it could be a great activity because it is a very famous musical.

## Héctor

> The story is centred on redemption and Christianity, and it is a great musical full of deep emotions.  
> I think it could give us a lot to talk about after the show.

---

## 9.2 Questions to Joel

## Héctor

> What do you think, Joel?  
> Would Harry Potter enjoy a musical like *Les Misérables*?

## Joel

> Yes, maybe. It could be interesting.

## Héctor

> Would you prefer going to the theatre, or doing something more relaxed?

## Joel

> I think the theatre is a good idea.

## Héctor

> Great.  
> Do you think Spiderman would enjoy the theatre, or would he prefer something more active?

## Joel

> Maybe Spiderman would prefer something more active, but he could enjoy it too.

## Héctor

> Yes, as an action hero, Spiderman would probably prefer something with more action.  
> But I think he could still enjoy the musical because the story is intense and emotional.

---

# 10. Final decision / Closing

## Héctor

> So, finally, we have decided to invite Harry Potter, Spiderman and Hermione Granger.  
> For the food, we will go to a Chinese restaurant and have tofu with vegetables and fried rice.  
> After the meal, we will go to the theatre to see *Les Misérables*.

## Héctor

> I think it is a good plan because the characters are interesting, the food has different options, and the activity is original.

## Héctor

> Do you agree with the final plan, Joel?

## Joel

> Yes, I agree.

## Héctor

> Perfect. I think we have finished.

---

# 11. Useful interaction phrases

## To agree

> I agree with you, Joel.

> Yes, that sounds good.

> I think that is a good idea.

> Yes, that could work.

## To disagree politely

> I see your point, but I’m not completely sure about that.

> That sounds good, but maybe we should think about another option.

> I understand your idea, but I think this option could be better.

## To ask for opinions

> What do you think, Joel?

> Do you agree?

> Would you prefer another option?

> Shall we decide the final option?

## To keep the conversation going

> Could you explain that a little more?

> Why do you think that?

> Maybe we should think about the characters too.

> So, do we agree on that?
